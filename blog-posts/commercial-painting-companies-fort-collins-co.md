## Hiring a Commercial Painter Is a Different Decision Than Hiring a Residential One

Most painting contractors can roll a bedroom wall. Commercial work is a different animal.

When you're dealing with stucco, EIFS, CMU block, metal cladding, or multi-story exteriors — and you need the job done without shutting down operations — the stakes are higher. The wrong crew costs you more than a bad paint job. It costs you time, liability exposure, and tenant or customer complaints.

If you're searching for **commercial painting companies in Fort Collins, CO**, here's what actually separates a contractor worth hiring from one you'll regret.

---

## 1. Crew Size and Project Capacity

A two-person crew that does great residential work is not set up to handle a 20,000 sq ft commercial exterior on a timeline. Ask directly:

- How many crew members will be on-site?
- Do you use subcontractors or your own employees?
- What's your current project load?

Legitimate commercial painters staff jobs appropriately and will tell you upfront. If the answer is vague, that's a flag.

---

## 2. Dedicated Project Management

Commercial jobs need a point of contact who isn't holding a brush. You should have someone managing schedule coordination, material ordering, subcontractor oversight (if applicable), and daily communication with your team or property manager.

Ask: *Who is my single point of contact, and what does their day look like on this project?*

If the answer is "the owner," make sure you understand what other jobs they're running simultaneously.

---

## 3. Surface Prep Knowledge for Commercial Materials

This is where most commercial painting failures originate — not the paint itself, but what happened (or didn't happen) before it went on.

Commercial buildings use materials that demand specific prep protocols:

- **Stucco and EIFS** require elastomeric coatings and crack remediation before topcoat
- **Metal substrates** need rust inhibiting primers and proper adhesion prep
- **CMU block** often requires masonry filler and specialty sealers
- **Previously painted surfaces** may need full pressure washing, sanding, or chemical prep depending on condition

Ask your contractor to walk you through their prep process for *your specific substrate*. A contractor who gives you a generic answer hasn't thought through your job.

In Northern Colorado specifically, surface prep matters even more. The UV exposure at elevation accelerates coating breakdown, and freeze-thaw cycles stress any surface with existing paint adhesion issues. A proper prep job is the difference between a coating that lasts and one that's peeling in 18 months.

---

## 4. Disruption Minimization Plan

Your tenants, customers, or employees don't care about your painting schedule. A professional commercial crew does.

Before signing, ask:

- Can you work in phases to keep entrances and parking accessible?
- What are your start/stop times, and can they align with our business hours?
- How do you handle dust, fumes, and debris control?
- Do you communicate daily with our on-site contact?

This isn't just a convenience issue — in some commercial environments (medical, food service, etc.) it's a compliance issue. A contractor who hasn't thought through operational disruption hasn't done enough commercial work.

---

## 5. Insurance Levels That Match Commercial Risk

Residential painters typically carry $300K–$1M in general liability. That's not enough for a commercial job.

Look for:

- **General liability:** $2M per occurrence minimum
- **Workers' comp:** Active policy covering their actual crew (ask for a certificate)
- **Commercial auto** if they're operating vehicles on your property

We carry $2M in liability coverage — not because it's a marketing line, but because it's the right level for the work we do. If a contractor can't produce certificates on request, walk away.

---

## 6. Local Experience in Northern Colorado's Climate

A commercial painting company that's done mostly work in a mild climate is going to underbid your prep and overestimate their coating's lifespan here.

Fort Collins sits at elevation. UV exposure runs 10–15% higher than lower-altitude regions. Winter freeze-thaw cycles stress substrates in ways that Southeast or Midwest contractors simply haven't encountered.

Make sure whoever you hire has a track record in this region — not just a license to work here.
